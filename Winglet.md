
# Winglet Design, Simulation, and Optimisation for a Boeing 737-800

---

### Task:

For my dissertation in the fourth year of my undergraduate degree, I was tasked with understanding the literature behind winglet design, then through the use of CAD and CFD, ascertain which design is most suited for a Boeing 737-800 and then optimise one of the winglet designs to improve its efficiency.

### Actions:

- Conducted a literature review on the field of winglets.

- Created a CAD model of a Boeing 737-800 wing in ANSYS DesignModeller, along with two winglet designs: a single blade blended winglet and, a double bladed blended split winglet.

- Created a mesh domain around the wing and ensured high mesh quality before conducting a mesh sensitivity study.

- Conducted a CFD analysis of the designs to compare the lift and drag efficiency of the designs.

- Conducted a parametric study of the single blade design to attempt to improve it’s efficiency by altering cant angle, and winglet taper ratio.

### Results:

- The single bladed blended winglet increased the lift of the wing by 3.31% and reduced the drag by 2.57%.

- The double bladed blended split winglet increased the lift by 6.07% and reduced the drag by 7.29% compared to the base wing, meaning this was the optimal choice for the Boeing 737-800.

- For each additional blade, the viscous drag increased however, this was offset by the reduction in Pressure drag.

- During the parametric study, varying the taper ratio and cant angle of the single blade blended winglet improved the glide ratio by 0.32, a significant improvement.
